# 👀 Judging Eyes

Ever feel like you're way too relaxed while browsing the internet? Do you sometimes wish someone was there to stare directly into your soul and judge your every click? Or maybe you just want a little desk buddy who lives in your browser and silently judges your horrible taste in memes. 

Well, look no further. **Judging Eyes** is a Chrome Extension that injects a pair of fully interactive, judgemental (or chill, or sleepy!) eyes right onto your webpage. 

## ✨ Features

- **Stalker Mode**: The eyes fluently track your cursor around the page in real-time. They know exactly what you're hovering over.
- **Dynamic Fatigue System**: They start off alert, but over time—if you keep browsing or leave them idle for too long—they'll start to droop and eventually fall asleep.
- **Fully Interactive Mechanics**: 
  - **Drag & Drop**: Don't like where they're floating? Grab them! They'll dangle helplessly in the air while you drag them, and then get annoyed when you drop them into their new corner.
  - **Poke to Annoy**: Click them to poke their eyes. They'll recoil and immediately run away to a new random spot on the screen.
  - **Meme Rage**: Poke them 4 times in rapid succession and witness their ultimate fury. (Spoiler: They turn into a wildly vibrating red meme face and blink out of existence for 10 seconds to cool off).
- **Customizable Moods & Models**: Use the extension popup to customize their vibe. Swap between Anime, Cartoon, Psychopathic, or Drunken models, and pick their core mood (Chill, Judgmental, Sleepy).

## 🚀 How to Install & Play

1. Download or clone this repository to your computer.
2. Open Google Chrome and type `chrome://extensions/` in your address bar.
3. Turn on the **Developer mode** toggle switch in the top right corner.
4. Click the **Load unpacked** button in the top left.
5. Select the repository folder containing these files. 
6. Boom. You're now being watched. Pin the extension to easily change models!

## 🛠 Under The Hood

Built entirely with pure, vanilla **JavaScript** (Manifest V3), **HTML**, and **CSS**. All of the fluid recoil, drag physics, dragging constraints, and meme animations are beautifully handled through custom CSS keyframes and vanilla `requestAnimationFrame` hooks. No heavy external frameworks were harmed in the making of this extension.
